SSAO (Screen-Space Ambient Occlusion)

SSAO, also known as Screen-Space Ambient Occlusion, is a trick that can surpass the ray-tracing process to make the corner area darker than the open area. Although sometimes the SSAOs are really subtle to observe, it does serves a great deal for the overall quality of the image. We choose SSAO as one of our add-ons since we think that SSAO adds a lot more detail to the traditional shadow-map and will make the image look more realistic. Actually sampling SSAO could be time consuming. In our implementation, lots of resources are taken by the SSAO sampling process (we are doing 100 samples per fragment) and at first the frame-rate does drop a little bit. Now it is a little bit better but overall SSAO is still a slow process.

As you can see, the difference between the images with or without SSAO mainly lies in the shadow part. More specifically, you can see a clear dark gradient under the teapot in the right image. This is making the image much more realistic than the left side one. In the second group of images, we can also see the SSAO under the spheres on the top of the pillows. That is very nice looking too. And at the bottom of the pillows you can also see a tiny little dark tint, also making the image looks great. But there is actually some bug of SSAO (since it's just a trick): the right side of the sphere on the right should not be tinted black that way though.

The last scene file is a random generated scene file that has a grid of cubes each with randomized size. This image is a great example showcasing the power of SSAO. As you can see the contrasts between the shadows and the surfaces become very big, making it better for the viewer to perceive the relationship of the space.

Our process of rendering SSAO is basically rendering the image in two passes. In the first pass we will run the SSAO shaders to generate an SSAO texture. And in the second pass, despite the normally rendering the image we multiply every pixel in the image with the SSAO texture we computed before. We already have different view modes setup for debugging the SSAO (since that process was a nightmare).
